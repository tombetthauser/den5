# Campground Options 🌲

These camping locations are within roughly **30 minutes to 2 hours of Sacramento** and were selected because they are more likely to allow **last-minute camping in March–April**.

For each site the following is included:
- Distance from Sacramento
- Approximate nightly cost
- Reservation requirements
- Spring availability likelihood

---

## Beals Point Campground – Folsom Lake State Recreation Area

Distance from Sacramento  
~30 minutes

Typical Price  
$28–$33 per night for standard campsites  
$48–$58 per night for RV hookup sites

Reservations  
- Reservations available through California State Parks
- Not required during off-season
- Some sites may be first-come-first-served outside peak summer

Season / Spring Availability  
- Open year-round  
- Very reliable option for **March–April camping**

Good For Scouts
- Large campground
- Bathrooms and showers
- Direct access to bike trail
- Easy logistics for multiple families

---

## Mineral Bar Campground – Auburn State Recreation Area

Distance from Sacramento  
~1 hour

Typical Price  
$10–$20 per night (primitive campground estimate typical for SRA primitive sites)

Reservations  
- First-come-first-serve
- No reservations required

Season / Spring Availability  
- Generally open most of the year unless storm damage occurs

Good For Scouts
- Riverside campground
- Very quiet
- Plenty of space for multiple families

Downside
- Primitive facilities (vault toilets only)

---

## Ruck-A-Chucky Campground – Auburn State Recreation Area

Distance from Sacramento  
~1 hour

Typical Price  
$10–$20 per night (primitive campground estimate)

Reservations  
- First-come-first-serve

Season / Spring Availability  
- Typically accessible in spring

Good For Scouts
- Remote feeling but still close to Sacramento
- Good hiking and nature exploration

Downside
- Rough access road
- Primitive amenities

---

## Finnon Lake Recreation Area (Private Campground)

Distance from Sacramento  
~1 hour

Typical Price  
$30–$45 per night (varies by site)

Reservations  
- Reservations recommended but often available on short notice

Season / Spring Availability  
- Open year-round

Good For Scouts
- Small lake
- Good for group camping
- Bathrooms and picnic areas
- Quiet and family-friendly

---

## Camp Nauvoo – Placerville Area (Private Campground)

Distance from Sacramento  
~1 hour

Typical Price  
$20–$40 per tent site (varies depending on group arrangement)

Reservations  
- Usually required, but group reservations can often be made with only a few weeks notice

Season / Spring Availability  
- Typically available in spring

Good For Scouts
- Designed for youth groups
- Multiple campsites together
- Good for campfire activities

---

## Dispersed Camping – Eldorado National Forest (Silver Fork Area)

Distance from Sacramento  
~1.5 hours

Typical Price  
Free

Reservations  
- No reservations required

Season / Spring Availability  
- Accessible most years in early spring depending on snow level

Typical Areas
- Silver Fork Road
- Ice House Road region
- Crystal Basin forest roads

Good For Scouts
- Very flexible
- Multiple families can camp together
- Campfires allowed when fire restrictions permit

Downside
- No toilets
- Must follow Leave No Trace
- Need to check seasonal fire restrictions

---
