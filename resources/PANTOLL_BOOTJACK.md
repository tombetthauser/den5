# Tentative Lion Den Camping Plan  
## Mount Tamalpais State Park (Bootjack / Pantoll Campgrounds)

This plan assumes a **small Lion den trip (3–5 families)** camping for **two nights (Friday–Sunday)** at Mount Tamalpais State Park.

Both **Bootjack Campground** and **Pantoll Campground** are:
- small walk-in tent campgrounds
- **first-come-first-served**
- about **15 sites each**
- **~$25 per site per night**
- open **year-round**, including March–April

The two campgrounds are **~1 mile apart**, making them ideal for a flexible plan.

---

# Primary Plan: Bootjack Campground

Bootjack is typically **quieter and more secluded**, which makes it a good choice for a Lion den.

## Friday Morning – Site Arrival
**Goal:** arrive early and secure 2–3 nearby sites.

Recommended arrival window:
- **8:30–10:00 AM**

Steps:
1. Drive to **Pantoll Ranger Station first**.
2. Ask ranger which campground currently has open sites.
3. Check **Bootjack Campground**.
4. Walk through the campground to locate **2–3 nearby sites**.
5. Claim sites by:
   - filling out iron ranger envelopes
   - paying **~$25 per site**
   - leaving gear/tents to occupy sites.

Suggested number of sites:
- **2 sites minimum**
- **3 sites ideal for flexibility**

---

## Friday Afternoon
As families arrive:

Activities:
- setup tents
- explore nearby trails
- simple snack / picnic

Good short walk:
- **Bootjack Trail loop**
- gentle forest hiking

Evening:
- campfire dinner
- simple camp games
- marshmallows
- early bedtime for kids

---

## Saturday – Hiking Day

Morning:
- breakfast at camp
- explain **buddy system**
- short group hike

Recommended Lion-friendly hike options:
- **Bootjack → Bootjack Trail Loop**
- **Bootjack → Mountain Theater**
- **Bootjack → Matt Davis Trail section**

All are **short, shaded forest hikes** suitable for young kids.

Lunch:
- picnic at camp or Mountain Theater

Afternoon:
- free play
- nature scavenger hunt
- optional short second hike

Evening:
- group dinner
- campfire stories
- stargazing if weather is clear

---

## Sunday – Break Camp

Morning:
- simple breakfast
- pack camp
- optional short walk

Possible stop:
- **East Peak summit area**
- panoramic views of the Bay Area

Depart by late morning.

---

# Backup Plan: Pantoll Campground

If Bootjack is full, proceed to **Pantoll Campground**, which is about **1 mile away**.

Pantoll details:
- ~16 walk-in sites
- first-come-first-served
- **~$25 per night**
- near ranger station and trail hub

Pantoll is usually:
- slightly busier
- easier to access
- still very good for scout camping.

---

## Pantoll Campground Weekend Schedule

The same weekend plan works almost identically.

Friday:
- claim 2–3 sites
- setup camp
- short hike

Saturday:
- hike from Pantoll trail hub

Recommended hikes:
- **Pantoll → Mountain Theater**
- **Pantoll → Bootjack Trail**
- **Pantoll → Matt Davis Trail**

Sunday:
- breakfast
- short walk
- depart

---

# Site Strategy for Scout Groups

Typical campsite limits:
- **6 people per site**
- **2 vehicles per site**

For a Lion den:

| Families | Recommended Sites |
|--------|------------------|
| 3 families | 2 sites |
| 4–5 families | 3 sites |

Bootjack and Pantoll together provide **~30 first-come campsites**, so arriving early on Friday gives good odds.

---

# Gear Considerations

Both campgrounds are **walk-in (short carry)**.

Bring:
- collapsible wagons or carts
- smaller coolers
- compact gear

Distance from parking:
- about **100–200 yards**.

Backup campground:
- **Pantoll Campground**

Arrival strategy:
- **Friday morning before 10 AM**

- **2–3 campsites**
