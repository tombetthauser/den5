# Possible Bonus Adventure Trips
This plan organizes Lion elective adventures into a small number of outings that allow a den of Lions (Kindergarten Cub Scouts) and their Lion adult partners to complete multiple adventures together.

Each section lists the **minimal official requirements** needed to complete the adventure according to BSA guidelines and clarifies when activities must involve a **Lion adult partner, den leader, or qualified adult supervision**.

---

# 1. Green Acres & Park Cleanup 🌿 
Location Example: Plant Foundry → nearby park

Adventures Covered
- Champions for Nature
- Ready, Set, Grow

Possible Activities
- Meet with a garden store employee to learn about plants
- Identify natural vs. man-made materials
- Learn about composting and recycling
- Park cleanup service project
- Lions select seeds or small plants
- Plant container gardens to take home

---

## Champions for Nature Adventure Requirements

These requirements may be completed with the **Lion adult partner, family, or den**.

1. Discover the difference between **natural resources and man-made items**.
2. Discover the difference between **organic waste, paper waste, and glass waste**.
3. Discover **recycling** and how it helps the environment.
4. Participate in a **conservation or service project**, such as a park or trail cleanup.

### Resources
- BSA Cub Scout Handbook: Lion Adventures section
- Leave No Trace Seven Principles (BSA-approved outdoor ethics guidance)
- Local municipal recycling guidelines (city or county waste management websites)
- BSA Outdoor Code:  
  *“As an American, I will do my best to be clean in my outdoor manners…”*

---

## Ready, Set, Grow Adventure Requirements

These requirements should involve the **Lion adult partner** and may also include the **den**.

1. Discover **where the food we eat comes from**.
2. **Plant a small garden** outdoors or in a container.
3. Visit with an **individual knowledgeable about gardening** (such as a nursery employee, gardener, or park educator) and learn basic gardening skills.

### Resources
- Cub Scout Lion Handbook
- USDA Kids Gardening resources
- Local garden centers or community gardens
- Cooperative Extension gardening programs

---

# 2. STEM Afternoon at the Church 💾

Adventures Covered
- Everyday Tech
- Gizmos and Gadgets
- Count on Me
- Build It Up, Knock It Down

Possible Setup
Create **activity stations** and rotate small groups of scouts.

Station Ideas
- Engineering (building towers and structures)
- Motion experiments (ramps, balloons, airplanes)
- Shapes and counting games
- Technology exploration

---

## Everyday Tech Adventure Requirements

These activities should involve the **Lion adult partner** or be done with the **den**.

1. Discover **technology around your home, meeting place, or neighborhood**.
2. Discover **ways digital technology can make life easier**.
3. Identify a **digital device used at home** and demonstrate to your Lion adult partner how you use it **safely**.

### Resources
- BSA Cyber Chip / Internet safety discussions
- Common Sense Media digital safety guidelines
- Parent supervision for device demonstrations

---

## Gizmos and Gadgets Adventure Requirements

These activities are completed **with a Lion adult partner**.

1. Explore **properties of motion**.
2. Explore **properties of force**.
3. Use household materials to **create a useful object**.

### Resources
- Cub Scout Lion Handbook
- Simple physics demonstrations (ramps, gravity experiments)
- Household materials (cardboard, tape, blocks, marbles)

---

## Count On Me Adventure Requirements

These activities may be completed with a **Lion adult partner or den**.

1. Make a **Lion using only squares, triangles, and circles**.
2. Play a **game based on counting or numbers**.
3. Organize items by **shape, color, and another category**, count them, and build something using one category.

---

## Build It Up, Knock It Down Adventure Requirements

These activities require a **Lion adult partner** and either **family or den participation**.

1. With your **Lion adult partner**, build a structure.
2. With your **den or family**, build a structure.
3. Build something **designed to be knocked down**.

### Resources
- Cub Scout Lion Handbook
- Building materials such as blocks, LEGO, cardboard, dominoes
- Adult supervision for safe building activities

---

# 3. Swimming & Biking in the Pocket 💦

Adventures Covered
- On a Roll
- Time to Swim

Activities
- Helmet safety discussion
- Bike ride on safe paths
- Swimming under supervision
- Buddy system practice

---

## On a Roll Adventure Requirements

These activities must involve the **Lion adult partner**.

1. Learn about the **safety gear used when riding a bicycle**.
2. Learn the **safety rules for riding a bicycle**.
3. Ride a bicycle **wearing proper safety gear and following safety rules**.

### Resources
- BSA Age-Appropriate Guidelines for Scouting Activities
- Local bicycle safety programs
- Helmet safety guidance from the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA)
- Parental supervision during rides

---

## Time to Swim Adventure Requirements

Swimming activities must follow **BSA Safe Swim Defense guidelines**.

1. Learn the **swimming safety rules**.
2. Demonstrate how to **enter the swimming area properly**.
3. With your Lion adult partner as your **buddy**, be active in water depths matching your ability for **20 minutes**.
4. Place your **face in the water and blow bubbles**.
5. Demonstrate how to **exit the swimming area properly**.

### Resources
- **BSA Safe Swim Defense** (official BSA safety training)
- BSA Guide to Safe Scouting
- Qualified adult supervision at swimming location
- Lifeguard-supervised pool or approved swimming area

---

# 4. Camping Trip 🌲

Adventures Covered
- Let’s Camp
- Pick My Path

Possible Activities
- Pack Cub Scout Six Essentials
- Short nature hike using buddy system
- Camp chores and teamwork
- Campfire games and storytelling

---

## Let’s Camp Adventure Requirements

These activities must involve the **Lion adult partner** and be part of a **pack or den camping activity**.

1. Learn about the **buddy system** in the outdoors.
2. Before the trip, discuss **expected weather and proper clothing**.
3. Pack the **Cub Scout Six Essentials**.
4. Attend a **Cub Scout overnight camp or pack/den campout**.

### Resources
- BSA **Guide to Safe Scouting**
- BSA **Age-Appropriate Guidelines for Camping**
- Cub Scout **Six Essentials**
  - Water
  - First Aid Kit
  - Flashlight
  - Trail Food
  - Sun Protection
  - Whistle
- BSA Outdoor Code and Leave No Trace principles

---

## Pick My Path Adventure Requirements

These activities may be completed with the **den or Lion adult partner**.

1. Explain that **choices have consequences**.
2. Perform a **Good Turn for another person**.
3. Learn the **rules of a game and play the game**.

### Resources
- Cub Scout Motto: **“Do Your Best”**
- Cub Scout Oath and Law discussions
- BSA character development discussions led by den leaders or parents

---

# Suggested Annual Schedule

Event | Adventures Completed
----- | --------------------
STEM Afternoon | Everyday Tech, Gizmos and Gadgets, Count On Me, Build It Up Knock It Down
Garden Trip | Champions for Nature, Ready Set Grow
Pool + Bike Day | On a Roll, Time to Swim
Camping Trip | Let's Camp, Pick My Path

Total Elective Adventures Completed: 10

Lions only need to complete **two elective adventures** to earn their rank badge, but completing additional adventures is encouraged and helps provide a balanced program including STEM, outdoor activity, service, and character development.
