# Sierra Buttes Day Hike / Dispersed Camping Info

![Upper Sardine Lake and Sierra Buttes](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/21/Upper_Sardine_Lake_and_the_Sierra_Buttes%2C_California.jpg)

Information and resources related to prospective scout-friendly day hikes and overnight camping trips to the Sierra Buttes area in Tahoe National Forest. This is a beautiful, high-elevation alpine area with lakes, forest trails, and an optional summit hike with incredible views. The goal is to keep this flexible, fun, and appropriate for the kids while still offering an “adventure” option for those who want it.

---

**Drive from Sacramento:** ~2 hr 15–20 min  
**Best months:** Late June – October  
**Cell service:** Very limited  
**Dogs:** Allowed (leashed in developed areas)  

---

# Day Hike Options

## Easiest – Sardine Lakes

- Distance: 1–2 miles  
- Elevation gain: 100–300 ft  
- Time: 30–60 minutes  
- Trailhead: *Sardine Lake Campground*

Great for all kids. Lakes, views, and very low effort.

---

## Easy/Moderate – Upper Salmon Lake

- Distance: 1.5–2 miles  
- Elevation gain: 200–400 ft  
- Time: 45–75 minutes  
- Trailhead: *Upper Salmon Lake Trailhead*

Short forest hike with a lake destination.

---

## Moderate – Deer Lake

- Distance: 3.5–4 miles  
- Elevation gain: 600–900 ft  
- Time: 2–3 hours  
- Trailhead: *Packsaddle Campground*

A good “challenge hike” that still works well for kids with breaks.

---

## Ambitious – Sierra Buttes Lookout

- Trailhead: *Packer Saddle Trailhead*

### Main Goal (Recommended for scouts)
- Distance: 4.5–5 miles round trip  
- Elevation gain: 1,400–1,600 ft  
- Time: ~2.5–3 hours  

### Optional Final Section
- Short but steep metal staircase (~180 steps)  
- Exposed (cliffside)  
- Completely optional  

We will treat the base of the stairs as the primary goal.

# Camping Options

## Option 1 (Best for families): Sardine Lake Campground

- Bathrooms: Yes  
- Drinking water: Yes (seasonal)  
- Tables / fire rings: Yes  
- Showers: No  

Reservations:  
- https://www.recreation.gov  
- Book 2–3 months ahead for weekends (fills quickly)

---

## Option 2: Packsaddle Campground (near Deer Lake)

- Bathrooms: Yes  
- Water: Usually not available  
- First-come or limited reservations  

---

## Option 3: Dispersed Camping (Most flexible)

Available near:
- Packer Lake Road  
- Butcher Ranch Road  

- No bathrooms or water  
- First-come, no reservations  

---

# Best Dispersed Camping Areas (Recommended)

## Packer Lake Road (Best Overall)

Use as your primary search zone:
- Enter: *Packer Lake Lodge* in maps  
- Drive toward: *Packer Saddle Trailhead*

Best section:
- Between Packer Lake Lodge → Packer Saddle

Why this works:
- Multiple established pullouts and clearings  
- Close to Sierra Buttes hike  
- Works well for groups  

---

## Butcher Ranch Road (Quieter)

Access:
- Turn off Packer Lake Road

Best section:
- First 1–2 miles

Why this works:
- Less traffic  
- More secluded  
- Good for smaller or quieter groups  

---

## Salmon Lake / Gold Lake Area (Backup)

Best section:
- Side roads near Upper Salmon Lake

Considerations:
- Fewer dispersed sites  
- More restrictions near lakes  
- Busier area  

---

## How to Pick a Good Site

Look for:
- Existing fire ring  
- Flattened, previously used ground  
- Space fully off the road  

Avoid:
- Meadows or fragile vegetation  
- Within ~200 ft of water  
- Blocking roads or gates  

---

## Arrival Tip

- Aim to arrive before ~3–4 PM on weekends  
- Good sites fill early  

---

# Dispersed Camping Rules (Quick Guide)

**Permits**
- No permit required  
- Campfire permit required for stoves/fires (free):  
  https://permit.preventwildfiresca.org  

---

**Where to Camp**
- Use existing pullouts or clearings only  
- Stay roughly within ~100–150 ft of roads  
- Do not drive off-road to create new sites  

---

**Where NOT to Camp**
- Within ~200 ft of lakes, rivers, or streams  
- In meadows or untouched vegetation  
- In posted “No Camping” zones (common near lakes)

---

**Stay Limits**
- Max 14 days per ranger district  

---

**Fires**
- Often restricted in summer  
- Use existing fire rings only  
- Always check current fire restrictions  

---

**Waste**
- Pack out all trash  
- Follow Leave No Trace practices

# Winter Conditions / Options

- Roads are not accessible in winter  
- Snow blocks:
  - Gold Lake Highway  
  - Packer Lake Road  

- Trailheads are not drivable

Winter access requires:
- Snowshoeing or backcountry travel  
- Long approaches  

Conclusion:
- Summer/fall destination  
- Not suitable for this group in winter

# Similar Hiking / Dispersed Camping Options (Within ~2–2.5 hrs)

## Donner Summit / Castle Peak (~1.5 hrs)
- Great ridgeline hiking with big views  
- Easy → moderate hike options (Castle Pass, Castle Peak)  
- Dispersed camping along forest roads  
- More crowded on weekends  

---

## Bowman Lake / Grouse Ridge (~2–2.5 hrs)
- Remote-feeling granite and alpine lakes  
- Excellent dispersed camping  
- Short hikes with high payoff  
- Rough dirt road access (slow driving)

---

## South Yuba River (~1.5–2 hrs)
- River hikes and swimming holes  
- Lower elevation (warmer, longer season)  
- Good dispersed camping nearby  
- Best for easier, more relaxed trips  

---

## Wrights Lake (~2–2.25 hrs)
- Classic alpine lakes and granite terrain  
- Mix of easy and moderate hikes  
- Developed campground + limited dispersed nearby  
- Some permit zones deeper into Desolation  

---

## Loon Lake / Rubicon Area (~2–2.25 hrs)
- Granite slabs and open exploration  
- Flexible hiking distances  
- Dispersed camping available  
- Some off-road vehicle activity  

---

# Key Takeaways

- Multiple hike options for different ability levels  
- Optional “big hike” without pressure  
- Flexible camping options  
- Dispersed camping is easy if you follow basic rules  
- Many similar areas within a short drive  
- Best experienced in summer/fall
